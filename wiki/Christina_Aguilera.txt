'''Christina Mara Aguilera''' (born December 18, 1980)  is an American singer, songwriter, actress, and television personality. Born in [[Staten Island, New York]] and raised in [[Rochester, Pennsylvania|Rochester]] and [[Wexford, Pennsylvania]], she appeared on the television series ''[[Star Search]]'' and ''[[The Mickey Mouse Club]]'' in her early years. After recording "[[Reflection (song)|Reflection]]", the theme for [[Walt Disney Pictures|Disney]]'s 1998 film ''[[Mulan (1998 film)|Mulan]]'', Aguilera signed with [[RCA Records]]. She rose to prominence with her 1999 [[Christina Aguilera (album)|self-titled debut album]] that spawned the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] number-one singles "[[Genie in a Bottle]]", "[[What a Girl Wants (Christina Aguilera song)|What a Girl Wants]]", and "[[Come On Over Baby (All I Want Is You)]]". Aguilera released two studio albums in 2001; the Spanish-language album ''[[Mi Reflejo]]'' and the holiday album ''[[My Kind of Christmas]]''.

She assumed creative control for her fourth studio album, ''[[Stripped (Christina Aguilera album)|Stripped]]'' (2002), which produced "[[Dirrty]]", "[[Beautiful (Christina Aguilera song)|Beautiful]]", and "[[Fighter (song)|Fighter]]". Aguilera's fifth studio album, ''[[Back to Basics (Christina Aguilera album)|Back to Basics]]'' (2006), debuted at number one on the [[record chart|album chart]]s of ten countries and spawned the tracks "[[Ain't No Other Man]]" and "[[Hurt (Christina Aguilera song)|Hurt]]". In 2010, Aguilera released her sixth studio album ''[[Bionic (Christina Aguilera album)|Bionic]]'' and starred in the film ''[[Burlesque (2010 American film)|Burlesque]]''. The following year, she debuted as a coach on the reality competition television series ''[[The Voice (U.S. TV series)|The Voice]];'' she appeared in six seasons of the show. Her seventh studio album, ''[[Lotus (Christina Aguilera album)|Lotus]]'' (2012), spawned the hit "[[Your Body (Christina Aguilera song)|Your Body]]". Aguilera has also been featured on several successful collaborations during her career, including "[[Lady Marmalade#Moulin Rouge! cover|Lady Marmalade]]", "[[Moves like Jagger]]", "[[Feel This Moment]]", and "[[Say Something (A Great Big World song)|Say Something]]".

Aguilera has been recognized as a pop icon and earned the title [[Honorific nicknames in popular music|"Pop Princess"]] in her early years.&lt;ref name="princess_pop"&gt;{{cite book|first1=Lee|last1= Bollinger |first2=Carole|last2= O'Neill|title= Women in Media Careers: Success Despite the Odds |publisher=[[University Press of America]]|year= 2008|page= 147|isbn=978-0-761841-33-3}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref name="abcnews"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://abcnews.go.com/2020/story?id=123689&amp;page=1|title=How Christina Aguilera Got 'Dirty'|publisher=[[American Broadcasting Company]]|accessdate=November 19, 2015}}&lt;/ref&gt; Her work has earned her five [[Grammy Award]]s, one [[Latin Grammy Award]], and a star on the [[Hollywood Walk of Fame]]. Throughout her career, she has sold more than 50 million records worldwide.&lt;ref name="papermag"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.papermag.com/christina-aguilera-transformation-2553214651.html|title=Christina Aguilera Is Back With a New Transformation|work=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|first=Marie|last=Lodi|date=March 26, 2018|accessdate=March 27, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; In 2009, she ranked at number 58 on ''[[Rolling Stone]]''{{'}}s list of 100 Greatest Singers of All Time, thereby becoming the youngest and the only artist under 30 to be named.&lt;ref name="RS"/&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.rcarecords.com/news/christina-aguilera-perform-american-music-awards|title=Christina Aguilera to Perform at American Music Awards|date=October 21, 2010|publisher=[[RCA Records]]|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151121223307/http://www.rcarecords.com/news/christina-aguilera-perform-american-music-awards|archivedate=November 21, 2015 }}&lt;/ref&gt; ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' recognized her as the 20th most successful artist of the 2000s, and ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' listed Aguilera among the top 100 Most Influential People in the World of 2013.&lt;ref name="BB_2000s"&gt;{{cite web|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111114051540/http://www.billboard.com/features/artist-of-the-decade-page-two-1004053061.story |url=http://www.billboard.com/features/artist-of-the-decade-page-two-1004053061.story |title=Artist of the Decade |work=Billboard |archivedate=November 14, 2011 |deadurl=yes |df= }}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref name="Influential"/&gt; Aside from her work in the entertainment industry, Aguilera is involved in charitable activities through human rights, world issues, and her work as a [[United Nation|UN]] ambassador for the [[World Food Programme]].

==Life and career==

===19801998: Early life and career beginnings===
Christina Mara Aguilera was born in the [[Staten Island|Staten Island borough]] of [[New York City]], New York, on December 18, 1980, to Shelly Loraine Kearns (ne Fidler), a musician, and Fausto Xavier Aguilera, a [[United States Army]] soldier.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|last1=Wang|first1=Julia|title=Christina Aguilera Biography|url=http://www.people.com/people/christina_aguilera/biography/|website=[[People (magazine)|People]]|accessdate=September 28, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; Her father is Ecuadorian, while her mother has German, Irish, Welsh, and Dutch ancestry.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite book|last=Dominguez|first=Pier|authorlink=|title=Christina Aguilera: A Star is Made: The Unauthorized Biography|publisher=Amber Communications Group, Inc.|date=December 10, 2002|location=|page=2|isbn=0970222459}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref name="Early life"&gt;{{cite journal|last1=Gregory|first1=Sophfronia Scott|last2=Ferguson|first2=Hayes|title=Uncorking the Genie|journal=People|date=September 27, 1999|volume=52|issue=12|pages=7576|url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20129327,00.html|accessdate=September 28, 2014|issn=0093-7673}}&lt;/ref&gt; Her family moved frequently because of her father's military service, and lived in places including New Jersey, Texas, New York, and Japan.&lt;ref name="WashingtonPost"&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/WPcap/2000-02/13/003r-021300-idx.html |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6NBFEkA2w?url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/WPcap/2000-02/13/003r-021300-idx.html |title=Christina Aguilera's Fast Track |last=Harrington |first=Richard |newspaper=[[The Washington Post]] |date=February 13, 2000 |archivedate=February 6, 2014 |deadurl=no |df=mdy }}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguilera and her mother alleged that her father was physically and emotionally [[child abuse|abusive]],&lt;ref name="mtv3"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/bands/a/aguilera_christina/news_feature_080906/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140805022136/http://www.mtv.com/bands/a/aguilera_christina/news_feature_080906/|archivedate=August 5, 2014|title=Christina Aguilera's Old Soul|publisher=[[MTV Networks]]|first=Jennifer|last=Vineyard|accessdate=July 21, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt; claims which he denied; Aguilera used music as a form of escape from her turbulent household.&lt;ref name="WMagazine"/&gt; Following her parents' divorce when she was six years old,&lt;ref name="Early life"/&gt; Aguilera, her younger sister Rachel, and her mother (who later remarried&lt;ref&gt;{{cite journal|last=Helligar|first=Jeremy|last2=Majewski|first2=Lori|date=February 3, 2003|title=Christina's World|journal=[[Us Weekly]]}}&lt;/ref&gt; Jim Kearns and gave birth to one more child, Michael 'Mikey' Kearns),&lt;ref&gt;[http://starschanges.com/christina-aguilera-family/ Christina Aguilera family: siblings, parents, children, husband&lt;!-- Bot generated title --&gt;]&lt;/ref&gt; moved into her grandmother's home in the [[Pittsburgh]] suburb of [[Rochester, Pennsylvania]].&lt;ref name="Early life"/&gt; After several years of being estranged, Aguilera expressed interest in reconciling with her father in 2012.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://b96.cbslocal.com/2012/02/09/christina-aguilera-wants-to-reconcile-with-estranged-father/|title=Christina Aguilera Wants To Reconcile With Estranged Father|publisher=[[CBS Local Media]]|date=February 9, 2012|accessdate=July 29, 2014}}{{dead link|date=March 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}&lt;/ref&gt;

Growing up, Aguilera, known locally as "the little girl with the big voice",&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.newsweek.com/christina-aguilera-comes-age-112341|title=Christina Aguilera Comes of Age|last=Ali|first=Lorraine|date=July 30, 2006|work=[[Newsweek]]|accessdate=July 29, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; aspired to be a singer, singing in local talent shows and competitions. She won her first talent show at the age of 8, in which she performed [[Whitney Houston]]'s "[[I Wanna Dance with Somebody (Who Loves Me)]]".&lt;ref name="WashingtonPost"/&gt; In 1990, she appeared on ''[[Star Search]]'' singing "[[A Sunday Kind of Love]]", and was eliminated during the semi-final rounds.&lt;ref name="WMagazine"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.wmagazine.com/people/celebrities/2011/07/christina-aguilera-cover-story|title=From the Vaults: The Fall &amp; Rise of Christina Aguilera|last=Hirschberg|first=Lynn|work=[[W (magazine)|W]]|date=July 2011|accessdate=July 29, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; She later performed the same song during an appearance on Pittsburgh's [[KDKA-TV]]'s ''Wake Up with [[Larry Richert]]''.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite book|last=Kimpel|first=Dan|title=How They Made It: True Stories of How Music's Biggest Stars Went from Start to Stardom|page=2|year=2006|isbn=9780634076428|publisher=[[Hal Leonard Corporation]]}}&lt;/ref&gt; Throughout her youth in Pittsburgh, Aguilera sang "[[The Star-Spangled Banner]]" before [[Pittsburgh Penguins]] hockey, [[Pittsburgh Steelers]] football, and [[Pittsburgh Pirates]] baseball games, in addition to the [[1992 Stanley Cup Finals]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://triblive.com/x/pittsburghtrib/sports/penguins/s_623827.html#axzz2iBMUrf9N|title=Anthem singer fires up Mellon crowd|last=Grupp|first=John|newspaper=[[Pittsburgh Tribune-Review]]|date=May 6, 2009|accessdate=February 5, 2010}}&lt;/ref&gt; She attended [[Rochester Area School District]] in Rochester and [[Marshall Middle School (Pittsburgh)|Marshall Middle School]] near Wexford, and briefly attended [[North Allegheny Intermediate High School]] before being home schooled due to bullying.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://ca.movies.yahoo.com/person/christina-aguilera/biography.html|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140812193158/https://ca.movies.yahoo.com/person/christina-aguilera/biography.html|archivedate=August 12, 2014|title=Christina Aguilera Biography|publisher=[[Yahoo! Movies]]|accessdate=July 29, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt;

In 1991, Aguilera auditioned for a position on ''[[The Mickey Mouse Club#1990s revival, The All-New Mickey Mouse Club (MMC)|The Mickey Mouse Club]]'', although she did not meet its age requirements. She joined the television series two years later, where she performed musical numbers and [[sketch comedy]] until its cancellation in 1994.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://music.yahoo.com/read/interview/12043048|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070401032341/http://music.yahoo.com/read/interview/12043048|title=Dreaming of Genie|last=Goldstein|first=Rob|publisher=[[Yahoo! Music]]|date=May 15, 1999|archivedate=April 1, 2007 }}&lt;/ref&gt; Her co-stars included [[Ryan Gosling]], [[Keri Russell]], [[Britney Spears]], and [[Justin Timberlake]].&lt;ref name="AguileraBiographyPeople"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/people/christina_aguilera|title=Christina Aguilera Biography|work=People|accessdate=December 8, 2010}}&lt;/ref&gt; When she was fourteen, Aguilera recorded her first song, the duet "All I Wanna Do" with Japanese singer Keizo Nakanishi.&lt;ref&gt;{{harvnb|Louise Golden|2000|page=14}}&lt;/ref&gt; She later sent her [[cover version]] of Houston's "[[Run to You (Whitney Houston song)|Run to You]]" to [[Walt Disney Pictures]] in hopes of being selected to record the theme song "[[Reflection (song)|Reflection]]" for their animated film ''[[Mulan (1998 film)|Mulan]]'' (1998).&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,84715,00.html |title=Club Kid |last=Willman |first=Chris |work=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |date=October 11, 2010 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150103052149/http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0%2C%2C84715%2C00.html |archivedate=January 3, 2015 |deadurl=yes |df= }}&lt;/ref&gt; "Reflection" peaked at number 19 on the U.S. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Adult Contemporary (chart)|Adult Contemporary Chart]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/299251/Christina%20Aguilera/chart?f=341|title=Adult Contemporary for Christina Aguilera|work=Billboard|accessdate=February 23, 2015}}&lt;/ref&gt;

===19992001: ''Christina Aguilera'', ''Mi Reflejo'', and ''My Kind of Christmas''===
After the recording of "Reflection", Aguilera attracted attention of [[RCA Records]] A&amp;R [[Ron Fair]] and was signed with the label quickly afterwards.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/the-christina-aguilera-story-so-far-19991028|title=The Christina Aguilera Story (So Far)|first=Anthony|last=Bozza|work=Rolling Stone|date=October 28, 1999|accessdate=January 19, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; The label then started presenting Aguilera with tracks to record and laid foundation for her debut album.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite journal|last=Smith|first=Andy|title=One talented teen|work=[[The Providence Journal]]|date=August 15, 1998}}&lt;/ref&gt; While initially catering to making Aguilera's debut a "wonder introduction of a singer" like [[Barbra Streisand]], RCA was pressurized by contemporary [[teen pop]] craze involving Aguilera's peers including Britney Spears, leading to the label rushing the production process and aligning Aguilera to be part of the teen pop trend.&lt;ref name="Dominguez"&gt;{{harvnb|Dominguez|2003|p=45}}&lt;/ref&gt; They decided the lead single off the album would be "[[Genie in a Bottle]]", a trendy [[pop music|pop]] and [[contemporary R&amp;B|R&amp;B]] track released in June 1999.&lt;ref name="Dominguez"/&gt; The single was a major commercial success, peaking atop the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and record charts of 20 other countries.&lt;ref name="GeniePeak21"&gt;{{cite journal|title=Portrait of an Artist: Christina Aguilera|work=Billboard|date=January 20, 2000|volume=112|issue=5|pages=67}}&lt;/ref&gt; It has sold over 7 million copies, becoming one of the [[List of best-selling singles|best-selling singles]] worldwide.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Christina-Aguilera/news-93194.html|title=L'histoire d'un tube : 'Genie in a Bottle' de Christina Aguilera fte ses 15 ans|publisher=Charts in France|language=fra|date=August 17, 2014|accessdate=January 20, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt;

Following the success of the single, [[Christina Aguilera (album)|Aguilera's eponymous debut album]] was released in August to commercial success, peaking at number one on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and was certified [[RIAA certification|eight times platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).&lt;ref name="Washington"&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/WPcap/2000-02/13/003r-021300-idx.html|title=Christina Aguilera's Fast Track|work=The Washington Post|date=February 13, 2000|first=Richard|last=Harrington|accessdate=November 20, 2015}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref name="RIAA"/&gt; It moved 14 million copies worldwide,&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.contactmusic.com/christina-aguilera|title=Christina Aguilera Biography|work=[[Contact Music]]|accessdate=August 26, 2017|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170707135806/http://www.contactmusic.com/christina-aguilera|archivedate=July 7, 2017}}&lt;/ref&gt; and produced three singles, including two ''Billboard'' Hot 100 number ones "[[What a Girl Wants (Christina Aguilera song)|What a Girl Wants]]" and "[[Come On Over Baby (All I Want Is You)]]", and an [[All-4-One]] cover of "[[I Turn to You (All-4-One song)#Christina Aguilera cover|I Turn to You]]".&lt;ref name="AguileraBBCharts"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/299251/Christina+Aguilera/chart?f=379|title=Chart history for Christina Aguilera|work=Billboard|accessdate=June 20, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; The album helped Aguilera win the [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist]] at the [[42nd Annual Grammy Awards|2000 ceremony]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://music.yahoo.com/read/story/12051721|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080528131603/http://music.yahoo.com/read/story/12051721|title=Christina Aguilera Wins Best New Artist|last=Rosen|first=Craig|publisher=Yahoo! Music|date=February 24, 2000|archivedate=May 28, 2008}}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguiera's two following studio albums ''[[Mi Reflejo]]'' and ''[[My Kind of Christmas]]'' were subsequently released in the end of 2000.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite journal|title=Charts and retail notas|journal=Billboard|volume=112|issue=45|page=47|date=November 4, 2000|first=Leila|last=Cobo}}&lt;/ref&gt; The former topped the [[Billboard Top Latin Albums|''Billboard'' Top Latin Albums]] for nineteen consecutive weeks and was certified six times platinum in the [[Latin music|Latin]] field by the RIAA.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140726142412/http://www.billboard.com/charts/2001-02-03/latin-albums|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2001-02-03/latin-albums|title=Top Latin Albums: February 3, 2001|work=Billboard|archivedate=July 26, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref name="RIAA"&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&amp;se=Christina+Aguilera |title=Gold &amp; Platinum  Christina Aguilera|publisher=Recording Industry Association of America |accessdate= January 20, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; It won the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Female Pop Vocal Album]] at the [[2nd Annual Latin Grammy Awards|2001 ceremony]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/christina-wins-latin-grammy-20011030|title=Christina Wins Latin Grammy|last=Saraceno|first=Christina|work=Rolling Stone|date=October 30, 2001|accessdate=December 4, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt;

In support of her albums, Aguilera embarked on her first concert tour [[Christina Aguilera in Concert]] from mid-2000 to early 2001, visiting North America, Europe, South America, and Japan.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1424807/aguilera-to-play-prom-in-augusta-georgia/|title=Aguilera To Play Prom In Augusta, Georgia|first=David|last=Basham|publisher=MTV News|date=August 21, 2000|accessdate=July 6, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20010330211536/http://www.christina-a.com/christinainjapan.html|url=http://www.christina-a.com/christinainjapan.html|title=Christina Aguilera Live in Japan|publisher=Christina Aguilera website|date=February 9, 2001|archivedate=March 30, 2001}}&lt;/ref&gt; During this time, Aguilera was involved in a romantic relationship with dancer Jorge Santos, which ultimately ended in September 2001.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://content.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,1000774,00.html |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131022174651/http://content.time.com/time/magazine/article/0%2C9171%2C1000774%2C00.html |title=What a Woman Wants |first=Elaine |last=Rivera |work=Time |date=September 15, 2001 |archivedate=October 22, 2013 |deadurl=yes |df= }}&lt;/ref&gt; Thanks to her success throughout 2000, ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' recognized Aguilera as the Top Female Pop Act of the Year.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite journal|title=The Year in Music 2000|journal=Billboard|page=58|date=December 30, 2000|volume=112|issue=53}}&lt;/ref&gt; Despite her early success, Aguilera was displeased with the music and image her management Steve Kurtz had aligned her to, feeling unable to control her own image.&lt;ref name="NotYourPuppet"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/bands/a/aguilera_christina/news_feature_feb_02/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090305024500/http://www.mtv.com/bands/a/aguilera_christina/news_feature_feb_02/|archivedate=March 5, 2009|title=Christina Aguilera: Not Your Puppet|last=Stitzel|first=Kim|publisher=MTV News|date=February 2002|accessdate=January 29, 2009}}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguilera's views of Kurtz's influence in matters of her creative direction, the role of being her exclusive personal manager, and over-scheduling had in part caused her to seek legal means of terminating their management contract. In October 2000, Aguilera filed a breach of [[fiduciary duty]] lawsuit against Kurtz for improper, undue and inappropriate influence over her professional activities, as well as fraud. After terminating Kurtz's services, [[Irving Azoff]] was hired as her new manager.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/story?id=114375&amp;page=1|title=Aguilera's Ex-Manager Fires Back|publisher=ABC News|date=October 19, 2000|accessdate=December 6, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt;

Aguilera's first take on more artistic control was expressed in the 2001 ''[[Moulin Rouge! Music from Baz Luhrmann's Film|Moulin Rouge!]]'' cover of [[Labelle]]'s "[[Lady Marmalade]]", which also saw the contribution of [[Pink (singer)|Pink]], [[Mya (singer)|Mya]] and [[Lil Kim]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.newsweek.com/christina-aguilera-comes-age-112379|title=Christina Aguilera comes of age|work=[[Newsweek]]|date=July 30, 2016|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180120135330/http://www.newsweek.com/christina-aguilera-comes-age-112379|archivedate=January 20, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; Originally opposed by RCA executives for being "too [[urban music|urban]]" and "too [[Rockwilder]] and [[Missy Elliott|Missy]]", Aguilera insisted on recording the song.&lt;ref name="NotYourPuppet"/&gt; "Lady Marmalade" topped the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 for five weeks based on airplay alone, becoming the first airplay-only track in history to remain on the chart's top spot for more than one week.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/lady-marmalade-mw0000424595|title=Lady Marmalade  Song Review|publisher=AllMusic|first=Jose|last=Promis|accessdate=January 20, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite journal|title=Hot 100 Spotlight|work=Billboard|date=June 2, 2001|first=Silvio|last=Pietroluongo|page=123|volume=113|issue=22}}&lt;/ref&gt; It earned a [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Collaboration with Vocals]] at the [[44th Annual Grammy Awards|2002 ceremony]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.sfgate.com/news/article/The-2002-Grammy-winners-2868670.php|title=The 2002 Grammy winners|work=San Francisco Chronicle|date=February 28, 2002|accessdate=July 7, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; Later in 2001, an album titled ''[[Just Be Free]]'' containing demo tracks recorded by Aguilera in 1994 and 1995 was released by [[Warlock Records]] without Aguilera's permission. She subsequently filed a suit against Warlock Records and the album's producers to stop the release.&lt;ref&gt;{{harvnb|Dominguez|2003|page=34}}&lt;/ref&gt; The two parties came to a confidential settlement to release the album, in which Aguilera lent out her name, likeness, and image for an unspecified amount of damages.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/A/Aguilera_Christina/2001/07/03/pf-742790.html|title=Disputed Aguilera album to be released|last=McGrath|first=Stephanie|publisher=[[Jam!]]|date=July 3, 2001|accessdate=May 25, 2007}}&lt;/ref&gt;

===20022003: ''Stripped''===
[[File:Stripped Tour CH - cropped.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Aguilera performing on [[The Stripped Tour]] in 2003]]

When planning her fourth studio album, Aguilera leaned towards a new artistic direction that she felt had more musical and lyrical depth.&lt;ref name="USAToday"&gt;{{cite news|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/music/news/2002-10-23-christina-aguilera_x.htm |title=Aguilera's image is 'Stripped' |last=Gardner |first=Elysa |newspaper=USA Today |date=October 24, 2002 |accessdate=October 19, 2013 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160301144224/http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/music/news/2002-10-23-christina-aguilera_x.htm |archivedate=March 1, 2016 |df= }}&lt;/ref&gt; She named the album ''[[Stripped (Christina Aguilera album)|Stripped]]'' and explained that the title represented "a new beginning, a re-introduction of [herself] as a new artist in a way".&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1458433/christina-stands-up-for-the-ladies-discusses-fathers-abuse/|title=Christina Stands Up For The Ladies, Discusses Father's Abuse|first=Jennifer|last=Vineyard|publisher=MTV News|date=October 30, 2002|accessdate=July 7, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; For the album, Aguilera served as [[executive producer]] and co-wrote most of the songs.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite AV media notes|title=[[Stripped (Christina Aguilera album)|Stripped]]|others=Christina Aguilera|year=2002|format=CD liner notes|publisher=[[RCA Records]]}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref name="Vice_Stripped"&gt;{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/article/d3dpby/christina-aguilera-stripped-gender-equality-15-anniversary|title=15 Years After Christina Aguilera's 'Stripped,' We're Still Nowhere Near Gender Equality|first=Sophie|last=Willkinson|work=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]|date=October 27, 2017|accessdate=January 20, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; The album was preceded by the single "[[Dirrty]]", which was released to shed Aguilera's teen pop image and express her sexuality and aggression.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/07/28/arts/28iht-chris.2322180.html?pagewanted=all&amp;_r=0|title=Aguilera Aims for Edgy, bur Richer, Sound|work=[[The New York Times]]|first=Lola|last=Ogunnaike|date=July 28, 2016|accessdate=January 20, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; Its accompanying music video generated controversy for depicting various sexual fetishes and concepts.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|last=Edwards|first=Tanya L|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1458275/20021022/aguilera_christina.jhtml|title=How Dirty Is 'Dirrty'? X-posing The Kinks In X-tina's Video|publisher=MTV News|date=October 23, 2002|accessdate=June 16, 2007}}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguilera's new image presented in the video started to overshadow her music,&lt;ref&gt;{{cite news|last=Tryangiel |first=Josh |url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,1376243-1,00.html |title=Welcome to my Bubble |work=Time |date=August 27, 2006 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071012211218/http://time.com/time/magazine/article/0%2C9171%2C1376243-1%2C00.html |archivedate=October 12, 2007 |deadurl=yes |df= }}&lt;/ref&gt; generating widespread criticism from both her peers including [[Shakira]] and [[Jessica Simpson]] and the public.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite news|last=Dark |first=Jane |url=http://www.villagevoice.com/2002-11-12/music/siren-wailing/ |title=Siren Wailing |work=[[The Village Voice]] |date=November 11, 2002 |accessdate=June 18, 2007 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20041209030356/http://www.villagevoice.com/issues/0246/dark.php |archivedate=December 9, 2004 |df= }}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref name="blender"&gt;{{cite web|last=Duerden |first=Nick |url=http://www.blender.com/guide/67285/good-bad-dirrty.html |title=The Good, the Bad and the Dirrty |work=[[Blender (magazine)|Blender]] |date=November 15, 2003 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090807060733/http://www.blender.com/guide/67285/good-bad-dirrty.html |archivedate=August 7, 2009 |deadurl=yes |df= }}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguilera defended her new image, explaining that "I'm in the power position, in complete command of everything and everybody around me. To be totally balls-out like that is, for me, the measure of a true artist."&lt;ref name="blender"/&gt;

The final cut of ''Stripped'' incorporated various genres from [[flamenco]] and [[contemporary R&amp;B|R&amp;B]] to [[rock music|rock]] and lyrically revolved around the theme of [[self-esteem]] while also discussing [[sex]] and [[gender equality]].&lt;ref name="rs_Stripped"/&gt;&lt;ref name="Vice_Stripped"/&gt; It was released in October 2002 to mixed critical reviews;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/stripped/christina-aguilera/critic-reviews|title=Critic Reviews for Stripped|publisher=[[Metacritic]]|accessdate=January 20, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; [[Jancee Dunn]] from the ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' praised Aguilera's vocals yet panned the album for its lack of musical concentration.&lt;ref name="rs_Stripped"&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/stripped-20021105|title=Stripped|work=Rolling Stone|first=Jancee|last=Dunn|authorlink=Jancee Dunn|date=November 6, 2002|accessdate=January 20, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; The album was nonetheless a commercial success, peaking at number two on the ''Billboard'' 200 and has sold over 4.3 million copies in the United States.&lt;ref name="AskBillboardCareer"/&gt; It was a major success in the United Kingdom, having sold 2 million copies and became the second highest-selling album by a female US artist of the 2000s decade, behind [[Norah Jones]] with ''[[Come Away with Me]]''.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite news|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/official-charts-flashback-2002-christina-aguilera-reinvents-herself-with-dirrty-at-number-1__21081/|title=Official Charts Flashback 2002: Christina Aguilera reinvents herself with Dirrty at Number 1|date=November 23, 2017|accessdate=January 20, 2018|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|first=Rob|last=Copsey}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite AV media |date=December 28, 2009 |title=Best-Selling of the Noughties |medium=radio news |publisher=[[BBC Radio 1]]/Official Charts Company}}&lt;/ref&gt; ''Stripped'' has sold over 12 million copies worldwide.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/news/story/10699242/scott_storchs_outrageous_fortune/print|title=Scott Storch's Outrageous Fortune|work=Rolling Stone|author=Tour|date=June 29, 2006|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080419055040/http://www.rollingstone.com/news/story/10699242/scott_storchs_outrageous_fortune/print|archivedate=April 19, 2008}}&lt;/ref&gt; The album was followed by four singles, "[[Beautiful (Christina Aguilera song)|Beautiful]]", "[[Fighter (Christina Aguilera song)|Fighter]]", "[[Can't Hold Us Down]]", and "[[The Voice Within]]". "Beautiful" received universal acclaim for positive portrayal of the [[LGBT community]] and was the album's most commercially successful single, peaking at number two on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20050418084316/http://www.glaad.org/media/release_detail.php?id=3359|url=http://www.glaad.org/media/release_detail.php?id=3359|title=Eric McCormack, Todd Haynes, Christina Aguilera, The Hours, Six Feet Under Honored at 14th Annual GLAAD Media Awards Presented by Absolut Vodka in Los Angeles|date=April 26, 2003|publisher=[[GLAAD]]|archivedate=April 18, 2005}}&lt;/ref&gt; It earned Aguilera a [[Grammy Award for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance]] at the [[46th Annual Grammy Awards|2004 ceremony]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2004/02/09/arts/the-grammy-award-winners-of-2004.html|title=The Grammy Award Winners of 2004|work=The New York Times|date=February 9, 2004|accessdate=October 20, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt; The song was later listed at number 52 among the 100 Best Songs of the 2000s by the ''Rolling Stone'' in 2011.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/100-best-songs-of-the-aughts-20110617/christina-aguilera-beautiful-20110617|title=Christina Aguilera, 'Beautiful'|work=Rolling Stone|date=June 17, 2011|accessdate=January 21, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt;

During the promotion of ''Stripped'', Aguilera cultivated a new image by adopting the [[alter ego]] Xtina, dyeing her hair black, and debuting several tattoos and piercings.&lt;ref name="CareerBiggest"/&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/people/christina_aguilera/0,,,00.html|title=Christina Aguilera: Five Fun Facts|work=People|accessdate=October 19, 2013|deadurl=yes|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131022140434/http://www.people.com/people/christina_aguilera/0,,,00.html|archivedate=October 22, 2013|df=mdy-all}}&lt;/ref&gt; She co-headlined the [[Justified and Stripped Tour]] alongside [[Justin Timberlake]] from June to September 2003 in support of ''Stripped'' and Timberlake's album ''[[Justified (album)|Justified]]'', before embarking on her solo [[The Stripped Tour]] until December.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/justin-timberlake-and-christina-aguilera-double-trouble-20030626|title=Justin Timberlake and Christina Aguilera: Double Trouble|first=Jancee|last=Dunn|work=Rolling Stone|date=June 26, 2003|accessdate=October 20, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.frontiertouring.com/index.php?action=retail&amp;form_name=view_retail&amp;merchant_id=595&amp;sectionID=|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120325202833/http://www.frontiertouring.com/index.php?action=retail&amp;form_name=view_retail&amp;merchant_id=595&amp;sectionID=|title=Frontier Touring: The Stripped Tour|publisher=Frontier Touring|archivedate=March 25, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguilera garnered media attention after attending the [[2003 MTV Video Music Awards]] in August, where she and [[Britney Spears]] kissed [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]] during their performance of "[[Like a Virgin (song)|Like a Virgin]]" and "[[Hollywood (Madonna song)|Hollywood]]".&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/madonna-magic-at-mtv-awards/|title=Madonna Magic At MTV Awards|last=Moody|first=Nekesa Mumbi|publisher=CBS News|date=August 29, 2003|accessdate=February 22, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt; Later that year, she was the host of the [[2003 MTV Europe Music Awards]], where she won an award for [[MTV Europe Music Award for Best Female|Best Female]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1480240/its-justins-night-as-christina-kelly-osbourne-fight-at-mtv-europe-awards/|title=It's Justin's Night As Christina, Kelly Osbourne Fight At MTV Europe Awards|first=Jon|last=Wiederhorn|publisher=MTV News|date=November 6, 2003|accessdate=October 20, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt; ''Billboard'' also announced Aguilera as the Top Female Pop Act of 2003.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite journal|title=The Year in Music 2003|journal=Billboard|page=68|volume=115|issue=52|date=December 27, 2003}}&lt;/ref&gt;

===20042009: Marriage, ''Back to Basics'', and motherhood===
[[File:Christina Aguilera Sanremo.jpg|thumb|right|upright|Aguilera performing at the [[Sanremo Music Festival]] in 2006]]

In 2004, Aguilera recorded a [[Car Wash (song)#Christina Aguilera and Missy Elliott version|revised version]] of [[Rose Royce]]'s "[[Car Wash (song)|Car Wash]]" with Missy Elliott for the animated film ''[[Shark Tale]]'', in which she was also a voice actress.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1491393/christina-aguilera-is-poisonous-in-video-with-missy-elliott/|title=Christina Aguilera Is Poisonous In Video With Missy Elliott|first=Jennifer|last=Vineyard|publisher=MTV News|date=September 24, 2004|accessdate=October 21, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt; Later that year, she and [[Nelly]] performed "[[Tilt Ya Head Back]]" at the [[2004 MTV Video Music Awards]];&lt;ref name="EW2004"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://ew.com/article/2004/08/30/highlights-2004-mtv-video-music-awards/|title=Highlights of the 2004 MTV Video Music Awards|first=Brian|last=Hiatt|date=August 30, 2004|accessdate=January 22, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; the song was subsequently released as a single from Nelly's album ''[[Sweat (Nelly album)|Sweat]]''.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite journal|title=Singles: Essential Reviews|first=Michael|last=Paoletta|work=Billboard|date=September 25, 2004|volume=116|issue=39|page=55}}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguilera also contributed vocals to [[Herbie Hancock]]'s 2005 cover of [[Leon Russell]]'s "[[A Song for You]]", which was nominated for a Grammy Award in the category of [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Collaboration with Vocals|Best Pop Collaboration with Vocals]] at the [[48th Annual Grammy Awards|48th ceremony]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.baltimoresun.com/entertainment/music/bal-grammys-winners0209-htmlstory.html|title=Complete list of 2006 Grammy winners|newspaper=The Baltimore Sun|date=February 9, 2006|accessdate=July 7, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; During this time, Aguilera started working on her follow-up studio album while embracing an image inspired by public figures of the "[[Golden Age of Hollywood|Old Hollywood glam era]]" including [[Marilyn Monroe]], [[Marlene Dietrich]], and [[Mary Pickford]], debuting blonde curly hair and retro-styled makeup.&lt;ref name="EW2004"/&gt;&lt;ref name="HelloMag"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.hellomagazine.com/music/2004/10/20/christinaaguliera/|title=Christina turns Hollywood pin-up in latest shoot|work=Hello!|date=October 20, 2004|accessdate=October 20, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt; In February 2005, Aguilera became engaged to music marketing executive Jordan Bratman after dating for two years.&lt;ref name="Marries"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,1131176,00.html|title=Christina Aguilera Marries|work=People|date=November 18, 2005|accessdate=October 21, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt; The couple married on November 19, 2005 at an estate in [[Napa County, California]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.msn.com/en-us/music/celebrity/christina-aguileras-highs-and-lows/ss-BBgQqMJ#image=9|title=Christina Aguilera's Highs and Lows|publisher=[[MSN]]|accessdate=February 23, 2015}}&lt;/ref&gt;

Aguilera's fifth studio album ''[[Back to Basics (Christina Aguilera album)|Back to Basics]]'' was released in August 2006. The [[double album]], according to Aguilera, was "a throwback to the 20s, 30s, and 40s-style [[jazz]], [[blues]], and feel-good [[soul music]], but with a modern twist".&lt;ref name="B2BMusic"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1533622/christina-makes-her-comeback-twice-as-nice-by-expanding-basics-into-double-lp/ |title=Christina Makes Her Comeback Twice As Nice By Expanding Basics Into Double LP |first1=Corey |last1=Moss |first2=Jeff |last2=Cornell |publisher=MTV News |date=June 6, 2006 |accessdate=October 22, 2013 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141012115058/http://www.mtv.com/news/1533622/christina-makes-her-comeback-twice-as-nice-by-expanding-basics-into-double-lp/ |archivedate=October 12, 2014 |df= }}&lt;/ref&gt; During the recording process, Aguilera was much inspired by works of classic blues and soul singers including [[Otis Redding]], [[Millie Jackson]], and [[Nina Simone]], which she described as "music that really had heart".&lt;ref&gt;{{cite journal|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=JhYEAAAAMBAJ&amp;pg=PA26|title=Old School|first=Melinda|last=Newman|work=Billboard|page=26|volume=118|issue=30|date=July 29, 2016|accessdate=January 22, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; The album was released to generally positive critical feedback, with compliments regarding its retro-oriented styles and Aguilera's vocals.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/back-to-basics/christina-aguilera/critic-reviews|title=Critic Reviews for Back to Basics|publisher=Metacritic|accessdate=January 22, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; It debuted atop the ''Billboard'' 200 and has sold over 5 million copies worldwide,&lt;ref&gt;{{harvnb|Davis|2013|page=496}}&lt;/ref&gt; of which 1.7 million copies were sold in the United States.&lt;ref name="AskBillboardCareer"/&gt; The album was preceded by "[[Ain't No Other Man]]", which reached number 6 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 and has sold 1.7 million digital copies in the United States.&lt;ref name="AskBillboardCareer"/&gt; The song's music video saw Aguilera portraying her new alter ego Baby Jane.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1534591/aint-no-other-man-video-has-christina-singing-the-blues/|title='Ain't No Other Man' Video Has Christina Aguilera Singing the Blues|publisher=MTV News|first=Jennifer|last=Vineyard|date=June 19, 2006|accessdate=January 22, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; The single won Best Female Pop Vocal Performance at the [[49th Annual Grammy Awards]], where Aguilera performed a rendition of "[[It's a Man's Man's Man's World]]" in tribute of [[James Brown]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2007/SHOWBIZ/Music/02/11/grammy.awards/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121021075431/http://edition.cnn.com/2007/SHOWBIZ/Music/02/11/grammy.awards/|title=A 'Nice' night for the Dixie Chicks|first=Todd|last=Leopold|publisher=CNN|date=February 12, 2007|archivedate=October 21, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt;

''Back to Basics'' was succeeded by two singles, "[[Hurt (Christina Aguilera song)|Hurt]]" and "[[Candyman (Christina Aguilera song)|Candyman]]". "[[Slow Down Baby]]" was released exclusively in Australia as the fourth single,&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://sonybmg.com.au/news/details.do?newsId=20030829004728|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070904081540/http://sonybmg.com.au/news/details.do?newsId=20030829004728|title=Christina Aguilera Arrives In Town This Week!|publisher=Sony Music Australia|date=July 12, 2007|archivedate=September 4, 2007}}&lt;/ref&gt; while "[[Oh Mother]]" was the fourth in Europe.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.radioandrecords.com/formats/charts/euro/Switzerland.asp|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071013015718/http://www.radioandrecords.com/formats/charts/euro/Switzerland.asp|title=Radio &amp; Records: Swiss Top 20 Chart|work=Radio &amp; Records|date=September 21, 2007|archivedate=October 13, 2007}}&lt;/ref&gt; In support of ''Back to Basics'', Aguilera embarked on the [[Back to Basics Tour]], commencing in November 2006 in Europe, visiting North America, Asia, and Oceania, and ending in October 2008 in the United Arab Emirates.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1540564/christina-aguilera-reveals-european-tour-next-up-us-clubs/|title=Christina Aguilera Reveals European Tour  Next Up, U.S. Clubs|first=Jennifer|last=Vineyard|publisher=MTV News|date=September 11, 2006|accessdate=October 23, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.koreatimes.co.kr/www/news/art/2008/11/145_5365.html|title=Christina Aguilera Shows Off Impressive Vocals|first=Cathy|last=Garcia|work=[[The Korea Times]]|date=June 25, 2007|accessdate=June 11, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.take40.com/news/12441/christina-to-tour!- |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140223032338/http://www.take40.com/news/12441/christina-to-tour%21- |archivedate=February 23, 2014 |title=Christina To Tour! ===20102011: ''Bionic'', ''Burlesque'', and ''The Voice''===

Aguilera's sixth studio album ''[[Bionic (Christina Aguilera album)|Bionic]]'' was released in June 2010. The album drew inspirations from [[electronic music|electronic]] genres, which had influenced Aguilera during her pregnancy.&lt;ref name="ElectronicBionic"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1641039/christina-aguilera-brings-new-songs-classic-hits-to-today-show/|title=Christina Aguilera Brings New Songs, Classic Hits To 'Today' Show|first=James|last=Dinh|publisher=MTV News|date=June 8, 2010|accessdate=July 8, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; Critical response to the album was mixed, with some of the critics did not welcome the change in musical styles as well as the content of the lyrics for being "cringeworthy".&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/bionic/christina-aguilera/critic-reviews|title=Critic Reviews for Bionic|publisher=Metacritic|accessdate=January 24, 2018}}&lt;/ref&gt; ''Bionic'' struggled to match the chart impact and sales of her previous albums, peaking at number three on the ''Billboard'' 200 and has sold 315,000 copies in the United States.&lt;ref name="AguileraBBCharts"/&gt;&lt;ref name="AskBillboardCareer"/&gt; "[[Not Myself Tonight]]" and "[[You Lost Me]]" were released as singles internationally, while "[[Woohoo (Christina Aguilera song)|Woohoo]]" featuring rapper [[Nicki Minaj]] was released as a single in the United States and several European countries,&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=http://www.fmqb.com/Article.asp?id=69239 |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6QujmdbEt?url=http://www.fmqb.com/Article.asp?id=69239 |title=Airplay Archive |work=FMQB |archivedate=July 8, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/be/album/woohoo-feat-nicki-minaj-single/id374246489 |title=Woohoo  Single

===20122017: ''Lotus'', second child, and television projects===
Aguilera's seventh studio album ''[[Lotus (Christina Aguilera album)|Lotus]]'' was released in November 2012. She described it as a "rebirth" of herself after the personal struggles she had overcome during the ''Bionic'' album era.&lt;ref name="CareerBiggest"&gt;{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/list/474045/christina-aguileras-top-10-biggest-career-moments?page=0%2C2 |title=Christina Aguilera's Top 10 Biggest Career Moments |work=Billboard |date=November 21, 2012 |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6QmnwPM1s?url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/list/474045/christina-aguileras-top-10-biggest-career-moments?page=0,2 |archivedate=July 3, 2014 |first=Jason |last=Lipshutz |deadurl=no |df=mdy }}&lt;/ref&gt; The album has become her least commercially successful album so far.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.com/music/thesound/a439874/what-happened-to-christina-aguilera.html|title=What happened to Christina Aguilera?|publisher=Digital Spy|first=Nick|last=Levine|accessdate=November 21, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt; It peaked at number seven on the ''Billboard'' 200 and has sold 290,000 copies in the United States.&lt;ref name="AskBillboardCareer"/&gt; The album was preceded by two singles, "[[Your Body (Christina Aguilera song)|Your Body]]" and "[[Just a Fool]]". During the promotion of ''Lotus'' in December 2012, Aguilera was temporarily replaced by [[Shakira]] for the [[The Voice (U.S. season 4)|fourth season of ''The Voice'']] and returned for [[The Voice (U.S. season 5)|the fifth season]] in September 2013.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/474926/the-voice-locks-in-fourth-fifth-seasons-on-nbc|first=David|last=Greenwald|title='The Voice' Locks In Fourth, Fifth Seasons on NBC|work=Billboard|date=September 25, 2012|accessdate=June 20, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.eonline.com/news/462458/the-voice-season-5-premiere-coaches-christina-aguilera-and-cee-lo-green-make-a-triumphant-return|title=The Voice Season 5 Premiere: Coaches Christina Aguilera and Cee Lo Green Make a Triumphant Return|first=Leanne|last=Aguilera|publisher=E! Online|date=September 23, 2013|accessdate=June 20, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt;

''Christina Aguilera'' (1999) prominently displayed [[teen pop]] and dance-pop music,&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/christina-aguilera-mw0000244313|title=Christina Aguilera  Christina Aguilera|first=Stephen Thomas|last=Erlewine|publisher=AllMusic|accessdate=July 9, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; while ''Mi Reflejo'' (2000) takes inspiration from [[Latin music (genre)|Latin music]].&lt;ref name="MiReflejoNYPost"&gt;{{cite web|url=https://nypost.com/2000/09/12/christinas-a-winner-in-spanish-too/|title=Christina's a winner in Spanish, too|last=Aquilante|first=Dan|newspaper=New York Post|date=September 12, 2000|accessdate=May 31, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt; Her first Christmas album, ''My Kind of Christmas'', combines covers of Christmas standards and several original dance-pop tracks.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/my-kind-of-christmas-mw0000100386 |title=My Kind of Christmas by Christina Aguilera on AllMusic |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6AeKy4Z9e?url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/my-kind-of-christmas-mw0000100386 |archivedate=September 13, 2012 |accessdate=September 13, 2012 |publisher=AllMusic |first=Stephen |last=Erlewine |deadurl=no |df=mdy }}&lt;/ref&gt; Aiming for musical diversity and artistic growth, Aguilera worked with  R&amp;B, rock, [[hip hop music|hip hop]], and Latin music for ''Stripped'' (2002).&lt;ref name="NotYourPuppet"/&gt;&lt;ref name="Stripped"&gt;{{cite news|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,384149~4~0~stripped,00.html |title=Stripped Review |author=David Browne |work=Entertainment Weekly |publisher=Time Inc. |date=November 1, 2002 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071012121321/http://ew.com/ew/article/0%2C%2C384149~4~0~stripped%2C00.html |archivedate=October 12, 2007 |deadurl=yes |df= }}&lt;/ref&gt; David Browne from ''Entertainment Weekly'' credited the release with distancing Aguilera from her pop contemporaries.&lt;ref name="Stripped"/&gt; Aguilera leaned towards R&amp;B, [[blues]], jazz, and [[Soul music|soul]] on ''Back to Basics'' (2006).&lt;ref name="B2BMusic"/&gt;&lt;ref name="LATimes"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2007/mar/07/entertainment/et-aguilera7|title=Props mistress|last=Powers|first=Ann|work=Los Angeles Times|date=March 7, 2007|accessdate=May 28, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref name="TheObserver"&gt;{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2006/aug/13/shopping.christinaaguilera|title=Christina Aguilera, Back to Basics|first=Paul|last=Flynn|newspaper=[[The Observer]]|date=August 12, 2006|accessdate=May 3, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt;

In contrast, ''Bionic'' (2010) sees significant use from futuristic [[electronic music]], described as an [[electropop]] album.&lt;ref name="ElectronicBionic"/&gt;&lt;ref name="AllMusic"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/bionic-mw0001989957|title=Bionic  Christina Aguilera {{!}} Overview|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|first=Thomas|last=Stephen Erlewine|authorlink=Stephen Thomas Erlewine|accessdate=August 8, 2014|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120711102448/http://www.allmusic.com/album/bionic-mw0001989957|archivedate=July 11, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9D05E6D71230F934A35752C1A9669D8B63&amp;pagewanted=2|title=For Gays, New Songs of Survival (page 2)|work=[[The New York Times]]|first=Alex|last=Hagwood|date=November 7, 2010|accessdate=August 16, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; The soundtrack for her 2010 feature film ''[[Burlesque (2010 American film)|Burlesque]]'' (2010) was influenced by ''Cabaret'', and featured several established songs that were redone as dance numbers in a fashion similar to ''Moulin Rouge!'' (2001).&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2007/film/news/screen-gems-enlists-antin-for-burlesque-1117974104/|title=Screen Gems enlists Antin for Burlesque|work=Variety|date=October 15, 2007|first=Michael|last= Fleming|accessdate=February 23, 2015}}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguilera partnered with mainstream producers including [[Shellback (producer)|Shellback]] and [[Max Martin]] during production of ''Lotus'' (2012); when discussing her interactions with the latter, she commented that "He's known about me but we haven't crossed paths," adding "Those records were the kind I wanted to stray apart from. If you look at what I did in the past (after my debut), I always try to do things that will challenge me and challenge the listener, too."&lt;ref name="Billboard Cover"&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/474983/christina-aguilera-billboard-cover-story|title=Christina Aguilera: Billboard Cover Story|last=Hampp|first=Andrew|work=Billboard|date=September 21, 2012|accessdate=July 29, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt;

Aguilera has commented that she feels a sense of responsibility to reference portions of her personal life so that "people that can relate might not feel as alone in the circumstance." Many of her works consequently discuss love; she has also co-written two tracks which dealt with the domestic abuse throughout her childhood.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/57547/basic-instinct|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090114034718/http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/57547/basic-instinct|title='Basic' Instinct|work=Billboard|date=August 13, 2006|archivedate=January 14, 2009 }}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguilera has frequently mentioned [[feminism]] in her music; she has expressed her belief that sexuality is "very empowering", and has commented that "If I want to be sexual, it's for my own appreciation and enjoyment! That's why I like to talk about the fact that sometimes I am attracted to women. I appreciate their femininity and beauty."&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20393540,00.html|title=Christina Aguilera: Why I'm Sometimes Attracted to Women|last=Oh|first=Eunice|work=People|date=June 11, 2010|accessdate=May 11, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt; She has alluded to her physical attractions to women in other interviews as well.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite news|last1=Stein|first1=Joshua David|title=Christina Aguilera Reclaims The Fame|url=https://www.out.com/entertainment/music/2010/05/16/christina-aguilera-reclaims-fame|work=Out Magazine|publisher=Here Publishing Inc.|date=May 16, 2010}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite news|last1=Bendix|first1=Trish|title=Christina Aguilera: What shes said about her sexuality|url=http://www.afterellen.com/people/80238-christina-aguilera-what-shes-said-about-her-sexuality|work=After Ellen|publisher=Evolve Media LLC|date=October 15, 2010|quote=Two women are way sexier than two men in bed. I definitely love women. I think theyre more attractive to the naked eye.}}&lt;/ref&gt; Her "Can't Hold Us Down" from ''Stripped'' denounces gender-related [[double standard]]s, in which men are applauded for their sexual behaviors, while women who behave in a similar fashion are disdained.&lt;ref&gt;{{harvnb|Railton|Watson|2011|page=88}}&lt;/ref&gt; The main theme of ''Bionic'' was also about woman empowerment; Hermione Hoby from ''[[The Guardian]]'' opined that "she incites more of a sisterly spirit of collaboration, including riot grrrl feminists [[Le Tigre]]. Aguilera's not shy of the odd feministic declaration herself."&lt;ref name="GuardianSexual"/&gt; In regards to production, Aguilera has often verbalized her preference of working with more "obscure" collaborators, and noted that she is not necessarily inclined to contact "the No.1 chart-toppers in music" because of their popular demand.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/58465/new-aguilera-album-set-for-august|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090114030752/http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/58465/new-aguilera-album-set-for-august|title=New Aguilera Album Set For August|last=Cohen|first=Jonathan|work=Billboard|date=June 11, 2006|archivedate=January 14, 2009 }}&lt;/ref&gt; ''The New York Times''{{'}} Kelefa Sanneh described her decision to work with [[DJ Premier]] on ''Back to Basics'' as "a decision to snub some of the big-name producers on whom pop stars often rely."&lt;ref&gt;{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/08/17/arts/music/17sann.html?_r=1|title=Honey They've Shrunk the Pop Stars|last=Sanneh|first=Kelefa|newspaper=The New York Times|date=August 17, 2006|accessdate=July 8, 2009}}&lt;/ref&gt;

==Image==
Aguilera has reinvented her public image on numerous occasions throughout her career.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://blog.chron.com//handstamp/2007/02/christina-aguilera-claims-her-pop-throne/ |title=Christina Aguilera claims her pop throne |work=[[Houston Chronicle]] |first=Joey |last=Guerra |date=February 20, 2007 |accessdate=July 17, 2014 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6WEW0UaKu?url=http://blog.chron.com//newswatch/2011/04/ktru-station-off-air-but-still-broadcasting-online/ |archivedate=February 10, 2015 |df=mdy }}&lt;/ref&gt; In the later 1990s, her then-manager Steve Kurtz marketed her as a bubblegum pop entertainer to capitalize on the genre's financial lure,&lt;ref name="USAToday"/&gt; which earned her recognition as a [[teen idol]].&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/shows/the_greatest/episode.jhtml?episodeID=67297 |title=50 Greatest Teen Idols |publisher=VH1 |accessdate=July 19, 2014 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120209143618/http://www.vh1.com/shows/the_greatest/episode.jhtml?episodeID=67297 |archivedate=February 9, 2012 |df= }}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguilera introduced her [[alter ego]] "Xtina" during 2002 and 2003, which presented an increasingly provocative persona.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/articles/2002/10/29/1035683410806.html|title=Aguilera sheds teen pop image, alongside clothes|date=October 30, 2002|work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|accessdate=July 17, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.virginmedia.com/music/features/alter-egos.php?page=6 |title=Christina Aguilera = Xtina |publisher=[[Virgin Media]] |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/6WYKGhfBU?url=http://www.virginmedia.com/music/features/alter-egos.php?page=6 |archivedate=February 23, 2015 |deadurl=no |df=mdy }}&lt;/ref&gt; During this time, she dyed her hair black, debuted several piercings, and participated in several nude pictorials for publications.&lt;ref name="CareerBiggest"/&gt; In 2004, Aguilera embraced a more "mature" image with retro-styled hair and makeup, which was inspired by classic movie stars including Marilyn Monroe, [[Marlene Dietrich]] and [[Mary Pickford]].&lt;ref name="HelloMag"/&gt; This reinvention was named "Baby Jane" in 2006, an alter ego which was taken from the 1962 film ''[[What Ever Happened to Baby Jane? (1962 film)|What Ever Happened to Baby Jane?]]''.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1539161/christina-clip-got-a-boost-from-outkast-role-playing-dancers-vmas-behind-the-camera/|title=Christina Clip Got A Boost From Outkast, Role-Playing Dancers|publisher=MTV News|first=Jennifer|last=Vineyard|date=August 23, 2006|accessdate=June 23, 2013}}&lt;/ref&gt; Aguilera received widespread criticism over her curvy figure from gaining weight in 2012.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/christina-aguilera-fat-girl-article-1.1171905|title=Christina Aguilera on her curvy, controversial figure: 'I got tired of being a skinny, white girl'|first=Joyce|last=Chen|work=New York Daily News|date=October 1, 2012|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141014134839/http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/christina-aguilera-fat-girl-article-1.1171905|archivedate=October 14, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; She later received favorable media attention after a significant weight loss in 2013.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web|url=http://www.ibtimes.com/christina-aguilera-weight-loss-how-did-she-do-it-1411294|title=Christina Aguilera Weight Loss: How Did She Do It?|publisher=[[International Business Times]]|first=Amethyst|last=Tate|date=September 26, 2013|accessdate=July 19, 2014}}&lt;/ref&gt; In 2018 Aguilera appeared bare-faced without makeup and [[photo manipulation|retouching]] on the cover of [[Paper magazine]] and received widespread praise.&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=http://www.thisisinsider.com/christina-aguilera-no-makeup-paper-magazine-cover-shaming-2018-3|title=People are praising Christina Aguilera for her makeup-free photoshoots|date= March 27, 2018 |work=INSIDER}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{cite web |url=https://www.cnn.com/2018/03/26/entertainment/christina-aguilera-no-makeup-paper-magazine/index.html|title=Christina Aguilera goes makeup free for Paper|date= March 27, 2018 |work=[[CNN]]}}&lt;/ref&gt;
