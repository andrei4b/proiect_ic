'''Taylor Alison Swift''' (born December 13, 1989) is an American singer-songwriter. One of the leading contemporary recording artists, she is known for narrative songs about her personal life, which have received widespread media coverage.
 
Born and raised in [[Pennsylvania]], Swift moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] at the age of 14 to pursue a career in [[country music]]. She signed with the label [[Big Machine Records]] and became the youngest artist ever signed by the [[Sony/ATV Music Publishing|Sony/ATV Music publishing house]]. Her 2006 self-titled [[Taylor Swift (album)|debut album]] peaked at number five on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and spent the most weeks on the chart in the 2000s. The album's third single, "[[Our Song (Taylor Swift song)|Our Song]]", made her the youngest person to single-handedly write and perform a number-one song on the [[Hot Country Songs]] chart. Swift's second album, ''[[Fearless (Taylor Swift album)|Fearless]]'', was released in 2008. Buoyed by the success of pop crossover singles "[[Love Story (Taylor Swift song)|Love Story]]" and "[[You Belong with Me]]", ''Fearless'' became the best-selling album of 2009 in the United States. The album won four [[Grammy Awards]], with Swift becoming the youngest [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] winner.

Swift was the sole writer of her 2010 album, ''[[Speak Now]]''. It debuted at number one in the United States and the single "[[Mean (song)|Mean]]" won two Grammy Awards. Her fourth album, ''[[Red (Taylor Swift album)|Red]]'' (2012), yielded the successful singles "[[We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together]]" and "[[I Knew You Were Trouble]]". With her fifth album, the pop-focused ''[[1989 (Taylor Swift album)|1989]]'' (2014), she became the first act to have three albums [[Lists of fastest-selling albums|sell 1 million copies within one week]] in the United States. Its singles "[[Shake It Off]]", "[[Blank Space]]", and "[[Bad Blood (Taylor Swift song)|Bad Blood]]" reached number one in the US, Australia, and Canada. The album received three Grammy Awards, and Swift became the first woman and fifth act overall to win Album of the Year twice. The 2015 [[1989 World Tour|concert tour for ''1989'']] became one of the [[List of highest-grossing concert tours|highest-grossing of the decade]]. Swift's sixth album, ''[[Reputation (Taylor Swift album)|Reputation]]'' (2017) and its lead single "[[Look What You Made Me Do]]" topped the U.K. and U.S. charts, with the former becoming her fourth million-selling debut.&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8039676/taylor-swift-reputation-album-sells-12-million-copies-us|title=Official: Taylor Swift's 'Reputation' Album Sells 1.2M Copies in US During First Week|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-05-29}}&lt;/ref&gt;

As a songwriter, Swift has [[List of awards and nominations received by Taylor Swift|received awards]] from the [[Nashville Songwriters Association International|Nashville Songwriters Association]] and the [[Songwriters Hall of Fame]], and was included in ''[[Rolling Stone]]''{{'s}} [[Rolling Stone's 100 Greatest Songwriters of All Time|100 Greatest Songwriters of All Time]] in 2015. She is also the recipient of 10 Grammy Awards, five ''[[Guinness World Records]]'', one [[Creative Arts Emmy Award|Emmy Award]], 23 [[Billboard Music Awards|''Billboard'' Music Awards]], and 12 [[Country Music Association Awards]]. Swift is one of the [[List of best-selling music artists|best-selling music artists of all time]], having sold more than 40 million albumsincluding 27.8&amp;nbsp;million in the USand 130&amp;nbsp;million single downloads. She has appeared in ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''{{'s}} [[Time 100|100 most influential people in the world]] (2010 and 2015), ''[[Forbes]]''{{'}} top-earning women in music (20112015), ''Forbes''{{'}} [[The World's 100 Most Powerful Women|100 most powerful women]] (2015), and [[Forbes Celebrity 100|''Forbes'' Celebrity 100]] (2016). Her inclusion in the third of these made her the youngest woman on the list, and she ranked first in Celebrity 100.

They began meeting for two-hour writing sessions every Tuesday afternoon after school.&lt;ref name="majorlyindie1"&gt;{{Cite news |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/liz-rose-songs-taylor-swift-little-big-town-20141024 |title=Songwriter Spotlight: Liz Rose |work=Rolling Stone |date=October 24, 2014 |accessdate=September 24, 2016 |last=Leahey |first=Andrew |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160926051735/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/liz-rose-songs-taylor-swift-little-big-town-20141024 |archivedate=September 26, 2016}}&lt;/ref&gt; Rose thought that the sessions were "some of the easiest I've ever done. Basically, I was just her editor. She'd write about what happened in school that day. She had such a clear vision of what she was trying to say. And she'd come in with the most incredible hooks". Swift was signed by the [[Sony/ATV Music Publishing|Sony/ATV Tree publishing house]],&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite web |last=DeLuca |first=Dan |title=Focused on 'great songs' Taylor Swift isn't thinking about "the next level" or Joe Jon as gossip |url=http://articles.philly.com/2008-11-11/news/25256467_1_big-machine-records-joe-jonas-country-music-association-awards |page=1 |work=Philadelphia Daily News |accessdate=April 17, 2012 |date=November 11, 2008 |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121118235847/http://articles.philly.com/2008-11-11/news/25256467_1_big-machine-records-joe-jonas-country-music-association-awards |archivedate=November 18, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt; but left RCA Records when she was 14.&lt;ref name="albuminfo" /&gt; She later recalled: "I genuinely felt that I was running out of time. I wanted to capture these years of my life on an album while they still represented what I was going through".&lt;ref name=":0"&gt;{{Cite news |last=Preston |first=John |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/rockandpopmusic/5202294/Taylor-Swift-the-19-year-old-country-music-star-conquering-America-and-now-Britain.html |title=Taylor Swift: the 19-year-old country music star conquering America  and now Britain |work=The Daily Telegraph |accessdate=August 30, 2012 |date=April 26, 2009 |deadurl=no |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120105153224/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/rockandpopmusic/5202294/Taylor-Swift-the-19-year-old-country-music-star-conquering-America-and-now-Britain.html |archivedate=January 5, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt;

Swift was at an industry showcase at Nashville's [[Bluebird Cafe]] in 2005 when she caught the attention of [[Scott Borchetta]], a [[DreamWorks Records]] executive who was preparing to form his own independent record label, [[Big Machine Records]]. She persuaded Big Machine to hire her demo producer [[Nathan Chapman (record producer)|Nathan Chapman]], with whom she felt she had the right "chemistry".&lt;ref name="albuminfo" /&gt; Swift wrote three of the album's songs alone, and co-wrote the remaining eight with writers Rose, [[Robert Ellis Orrall]], Brian Maher, and [[Angelo Petraglia]].&lt;ref name="liner"&gt;{{Cite AV media notes |title=Taylor Swift |others=Taylor Swift |year=2006 |type=CD |publisher=[[Big Machine Records]] |id=BMR120702}}&lt;/ref&gt; === 20102014: ''Speak Now'' and ''Red'' ===
In August 2010, Swift released "[[Mine (Taylor Swift song)|Mine]]", the lead single from her third studio album, ''[[Speak Now]].'' It entered the United States at number three, making Swift the second female artist in the history of the Hot 100 (after [[Mariah Carey]]) to debut multiple tracks in the top five in one year; the other was "Today Was a Fairytale" at number two.&lt;ref name="Billboard"&gt;{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/956910/taylor-swift-makes-sparkling-hot-100-entrance|title=Taylor Swift Makes Sparkling Hot 100 Entrance|last=Pietroluongo|first=Silvio|date=August 11, 2010|work=Billboard|accessdate=July 25, 2016|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160131095813/http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/956910/taylor-swift-makes-sparkling-hot-100-entrance|archivedate=January 31, 2016}}&lt;/ref&gt; ''Speak Now'', released on October 25, 2010,&lt;ref name="ibtimes" /&gt; was a commercial success, debuting at number one on the ''Billboard'' 200, and becoming the 16th album to achieve opening week sales of one million copies.&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite web|last1=Kaufman|first1=Gil|title=Taylor Swift's Speak Now Tops 1 Million in First Week|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1651391/taylor-swifts-speak-now-tops-1-million-in-first-week/|publisher=MTV News|accessdate=August 8, 2016|date=November 3, 2010|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160810044012/http://www.mtv.com/news/1651391/taylor-swifts-speak-now-tops-1-million-in-first-week/|archivedate=August 10, 2016}}&lt;/ref&gt; It became the fastest-selling digital album by a female artist, with 278,000 downloads in a week, earning Swift an entry in the 2010 ''[[Guinness World Records]].'' She earned another Guinness World Record entry after she became the first woman to achieve 10 track debuts on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite news|title=Fastest-selling digital album in the US by a female artist|url=http://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/world-records/fastest-selling-digital-album-in-the-us-by-a-female-artist/|accessdate=June 16, 2015|work=[[Guinness World Records]]|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150622164555/http://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/world-records/fastest-selling-digital-album-in-the-us-by-a-female-artist/|archivedate=June 22, 2015}}&lt;/ref&gt;&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite news|title=Most simultaneous US hot 100 hits by a female|url=http://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/world-records/most-simultaneous-us-hot-100-hits-by-a-female/|accessdate=June 16, 2015|work=Guinness World Records|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150622164648/http://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/world-records/most-simultaneous-us-hot-100-hits-by-a-female/|archivedate=June 22, 2015}}&lt;/ref&gt; Three of the album's singles, "Mine", "[[Back to December]]", and "[[Mean (Taylor Swift song)|Mean]]", peaked in the top ten in Canada.&lt;ref name="canada" /&gt;

Swift uses her life experiences as an inspiration in her work.&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052702304510704575562401576663866|title=Taylor Swift's Solo Act|last=Farley|first=Christopher John|date=October 22, 2010|newspaper=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|accessdate=May 24, 2012|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150201211555/http://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052702304510704575562401576663866|archivedate=February 1, 2015}}&lt;/ref&gt; In her songs, Swift often addresses the "anonymous crushes of her high school years" and celebrities.&lt;ref name="nytimes1"&gt;{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/10/24/arts/music/24swift.html?pagewanted=all|title=Taylor Swift Is Angry, Darn It|last=Caramanica|first=Jon|date=October 20, 2010|newspaper=The New York Times|accessdate=July 2, 2012|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120911131428/http://www.nytimes.com/2010/10/24/arts/music/24swift.html?pagewanted=all|archivedate=September 11, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt; Swift frequently criticizes ex-boyfriends,&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/john-mayer-taylor-swifts-dear-john-song-humiliated-me-20120606|title=John Mayer: Taylor Swift's 'Dear John' Song 'Humiliated Me'|date=June 6, 2012|work=Rolling Stone|accessdate=June 24, 2012|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120623203359/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/john-mayer-taylor-swifts-dear-john-song-humiliated-me-20120606|archivedate=June 23, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt; an aspect of her songwriting downplayed by ''The Village Voice'': "Being told What Songs Mean is like having a really pushy professor. In ''Fearless'', Swift featured fairy tale imagery and explored the disconnect "between fairy tales and the reality of love".&lt;ref&gt;{{Cite news |last=Kelly |first=James |url=http://www.accessatlanta.com/news/entertainment/music/taylor-swift-writing-her-own-songs-and-rules/nQy5n/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140908123045/http://www.accessatlanta.com/news/entertainment/music/taylor-swift-writing-her-own-songs-and-rules/nQy5n/|archivedate=September 8, 2014 |title=Taylor Swift writing her own songs and rules|work=The Atlanta Journal-Constitution |date=August 26, 2009 |accessdate=July 30, 2012}}&lt;/ref&gt; alienation, fame, and career ambitions.&lt;ref name="sessionsinterview"&gt;{{Cite web |url=http://music.aol.com/sessions/taylor-swift-change-sessions/ |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121009010116/http://music.aol.com/sessions/taylor-swift-change-sessions/|archivedate=October 9, 2012|title=Taylor Swift Sessions Interview |first=Melinda |last=Newman |date=December 19, 2008 |publisher=[[AOL]] |accessdate=March 25, 2011}}&lt;/ref&gt;
